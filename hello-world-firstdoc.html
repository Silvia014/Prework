French Polynesia

About the five archipelagos

  • Society Islands: The most populated and visited group, comprising 14 islands (including Tahiti and Bora Bora).
  • Tuamotu Archipelago: An expansive chain featuring 76 low, flat coral atolls.
  • Marquesas Islands: A stunning, rugged group of 12 islands known for towering cliffs and rich cultural heritage.
  • Austral Islands (Tubuai): A chain of volcanic islands located at the southernmost tip of the territory.
  • Gambier Islands: Located at the southeastern edge of the Tuamotus, this small group consists of four large volcanic islands and a few islets.

Do people live in the French Polynese? Here is a quick look at where and how locals live:

  1. The population is heavily concentrated in the Society Islands. The capital city is Pape'ete, located on the island of Tahiti, which acts as the economic and cultural center.
  2. The majority of the population (about 80%) is of Polynesian descent, alongside residents of European and Chinese ancestry.
  3. Locals are French citizens who enjoy a mix of French and traditional Polynesian cultural influences. The local economy relies heavily on tourism (especially in places like Bora Bora), agriculture, and pearl farming.
  4. Life is often characterized by a relaxed "island time," but residents also face challenges like a higher cost of living and isolation from global supply chains.
